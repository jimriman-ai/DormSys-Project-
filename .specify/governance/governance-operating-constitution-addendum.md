# Governance Operating Addendum
Status: Consolidated Operating Control
Purpose: Provide one compact operational statement of DormSys governance discipline
Scope: Human and AI governance participants

## 1. Governing Statement

DormSys governance operates on explicit authority, explicit scope, explicit blockers, explicit stage definitions, and explicit human adoption.

Nothing becomes authority by implication.

## 2. Absolute Operating Rules

- Authority is read only from canonical authority records.
- Planning artifacts do not grant authority.
- Artifact existence does not equal approval.
- AI is advisory only.
- Undefined terminology has no governance force.
- New stages require formal adoption.
- Reviews are not gates unless explicitly defined as gates.
- Freeze changes scope, not governance existence.
- Upstream governance may not be re-entered without authority.
- Blockers require explicit record, owner, and exit condition.
- Evidence comes before interpretation.
- Each governance decision uses one canonical authority source.
- Governance change requires human adoption.

## 3. Operational Halt Conditions

Halt immediately if:
- authority source is ambiguous,
- execution permission is inferred rather than explicit,
- a non-canonical stage is introduced,
- a blocker lacks record fields,
- a review is being used as a gate without definition,
- multiple authority sources compete,
- an AI summary is being treated as a governance record.

## 4. Control Outcome

When halted:
- run reality check,
- record retrospective if incident exists,
- classify any proposed rule,
- do not proceed by intuition.
